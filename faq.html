Questions

Before you buy

What is a vetiver window hanging actually for?

You hang it — at an entrance, in a bedroom, in the pooja room, in a car or at a workspace. Misted with water it releases a cool, earthy aroma and freshens the air. Indian homes and temples have used vetiver root this way for over 3,000 years.

Is vetiver the same thing as khus?

Yes. Vetiver (Chrysopogon zizanioides) is called khus in North India and vettiver in Tamil — the word means "the root that is dug up". It's the same plant used in khus curtains, desert-cooler pads and Ayurvedic cooling preparations.

How long does the aroma really last?

Up to 36 months. It isn't a constant perfume — the scent is strongest right after you mist it, which is the traditional method and reactivates the fragrance each time.

How do I care for it?

Mist lightly, don't soak. Let air move around it. Keep it out of continuous direct sunlight. There's nothing else to do and nothing to refill.

Where do you ship, and how long does it take?

Across India, free. Orders leave Dindigul, Tamil Nadu within 2 working days and usually arrive in 3–7 days depending on your pin code. You'll get a tracking link by SMS and email.

Is it safe around children and pets?

It's dried grass root and cotton cord — nothing is added to it, no chemicals, no synthetic fragrance, no plastic. Hang it out of reach of small children as you would any hanging object.
