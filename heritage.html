Three thousand years

Older than the fan.
Older than the wall socket.

Vetiver isn't a wellness trend that found a heritage story. It's a heritage that the wellness industry is only now catching up to.

Sangam era · c. 300 BCE

The root that is dug up

Old Tamil literature records vetiver's medicinal use. The name itself — vettiver — means exactly that: the root you dig up.

c. 100 BCE

Ushira, in the Ayurvedic canon

Classified as sheeta virya — cooling in potency. Prescribed against fever and the burning of the skin.

16th century

Khus ki tatti

Woven root screens hung in doorways and wetted through the afternoon. "When water is thrown on it," a historian of the period wrote, "winter seems to arrive in the midst of summer."

Then, and still

Garlands for the deity

Vetiver garlands adorn the murti of Nataraja in temples across the south, and are among the offerings most favoured by Ganesha.

Today · Ottanchatharam

The same root, the same hands

Harvested from independent farmers, cleaned, sun-dried, and hand-tied by rural women artisans. Nothing about the method has needed improving.
